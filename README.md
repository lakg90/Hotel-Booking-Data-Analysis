This project analyses a real-world **hotel booking dataset** to uncover patterns in customer behaviour, cancellations, pricing, and demand. The aim is to apply data analysis techniques to extract meaningful insights relevant to hotel revenue management and operations.

---

## Dataset Overview

The dataset contains booking information for city and resort hotels, including:
- Lead time and length of stay
- Number of guests
- Booking cancellations
- Room types and meal plans
- Pricing and seasonal effects

---

## Project Objectives

- Explore booking and cancellation behaviour  
- Identify factors associated with high cancellation rates  
- Compare city hotels vs resort hotels  
- Analyse pricing trends and seasonal demand  
- Apply data cleaning and exploratory data analysis techniques  

---

## Methods Used

- Data cleaning and preprocessing  
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)  
- Summary statistics and visualisation  
- Grouped comparisons and trend analysis  

All analysis was carried out using **Python**, with libraries such as:
- NumPy  
- Pandas  
- Matplotlib / Seaborn  

---

## Key Findings

- Longer lead times are associated with higher cancellation rates  
- City hotels experience more frequent cancellations than resort hotels  
- Prices vary strongly with season and hotel type  
- Certain customer segments show predictable booking behaviour
